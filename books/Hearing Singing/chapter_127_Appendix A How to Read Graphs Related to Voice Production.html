Appendix A How to Read Graphs Related to Voice Production Throughout this book, and indeed much of the voice pedagogy and vocology literature, you will encounter graphs. An author may include them to illustrate an important idea captured in the prose, or they may serve as a reference that you will extract different information from on each visit. Graphs are incredibly compact ways to store information and can either clarify or obfuscate depending on your ability to read them. Or, put in actionable terms, your ability to read them affects your ability to extract important information. This appendix will briefly explore the three most common types of graphs that appear in this book: the waveform, the spectrum, and the spectrogram.
